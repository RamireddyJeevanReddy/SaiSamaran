Daily Aarti's

Kakad Aarti

(7:00AM)

The Kakad Aarti, also known as the Morning Aarti, is a revered daily ritual in the worship of Sai Baba of Shirdi. This ritual takes place at the break of dawn, serving as a symbolic awakening of both the deity and the devotees to a new day. As the first rays of sunlight grace the horizon, devotees gather at Sai Baba's temple with offerings of flowers, incense, and devotion. The Aarti begins with melodious bhajans and devotional songs sung in praise of Sai Baba, accompanied by musical instruments. During the ritual, devotees offer lit oil lamps, symbolizing the dispelling of darkness and the illumination of knowledge. Chants and mantras dedicated to Sai Baba are recited, invoking his blessings and protection for the day ahead. The Kakad Aarti is not just a spiritual practice but a profound connection with Sai Baba's divine presence, emphasizing the importance of gratitude and seeking divine guidance at the start of each day.

Madhyana Aarti

(12:00PM)

The Madhyana Aarti, also known as the Noon Aarti, is a significant daily ritual dedicated to Sai Baba of Sai Smaran. This ceremony takes place around noon, marking the midpoint of the day and offering devotees an opportunity to reconnect with their spiritual devotion. Devotees gather at the temple, carrying offerings of flowers, incense, and reverence.The Madhyana Aarti commences with soul-stirring bhajans and devotional songs that glorify Sai Baba's divine presence. These melodious tunes, accompanied by musical instruments, create an atmosphere of deep spiritual connection. As part of the ritual, devotees offer flowers, incense, and a lit oil lamp to Sai Baba's idol or image, symbolizing their devotion and the dispelling of darkness with the light of spiritual knowledge.Beyond its religious significance, the Madhyana Aarti fosters a sense of unity and community among Sai Baba's devotees.

Dhoop Aarti

(6:15PM)

The Dhoop Aarti, also known as the Evening Aarti, is a significant daily ritual dedicated to Sai Baba of Sai Smaran. This serene ceremony takes place in the early evening, as the sun begins to set, enveloping the devotees in a tranquil ambiance perfect for reconnecting with their spiritual devotion. Devotees gather at the temple, each carrying offerings of fragrant incense, flowers, and deep reverence. The Dhoop Aarti is a soul-stirring experience, marked by the melodious rendition of bhajans and devotional songs that pay homage to the divine presence of Sai Baba. These enchanting tunes, often accompanied by musical instruments, evoke a profound sense of spiritual connection and devotion. As part of this ritual, devotees lovingly present flowers, incense, and a radiant oil lamp before the sacred idol or image of Sai Baba.

Shej Aarti

(8:30PM)

The Shej Aarti, also known as the Night Aarti, is a poignant and tranquil daily ritual dedicated to Sai Baba of Sai Smaran. This serene ceremony takes place in the late evening, as the day gently transitions into night, offering devotees a sacred opportunity to conclude their day with deep spiritual devotion. Devotees gather at the temple, carrying offerings of fragrant incense, flowers, and unwavering reverence. The Shej Aarti is a profoundly serene experience, marked by the melodious rendition of bhajans and devotional songs that pay heartfelt homage to the divine presence of Sai Baba. These enchanting melodies, often accompanied by musical instruments, create an atmosphere of profound spiritual connection and devotion.




Thursday Special

Along with all the aati's we will have special Pallaki Seva after Dhoop aarti.The Sai Baba Pallaki Seva, a cherished tradition in the worship of Sai Baba at Sai Smaran, is a grand procession that carries the divine presence of Sai Baba through the streets, symbolizing His benevolence and accessibility to all. This sacred ceremony occurs with great reverence and devotion, uniting devotees in a joyous celebration of their beloved deity. The Pallaki Seva commences with the preparations of the beautifully adorned palanquin, or "pallaki," that carries the idol or image of Sai Baba. Devotees lovingly decorate the pallaki with fragrant flowers, colorful draperies, and intricate designs, infusing it with a sense of grandeur and devotion. The rhythmic beats of drums, the melodious tunes of devotional songs, and the resonance of conch shells mark the beginning of this auspicious event. Devotees from all walks of life gather in great numbers, eager to participate in the procession. They take turns carrying the pallaki on their shoulders, a privilege that fills their hearts with profound joy and spiritual fulfillment. As the procession moves through the streets, devotees sing bhajans and chant mantras dedicated to Sai Baba, spreading His divine blessings and teachings to all who witness the procession.The Pallaki Seva serves as a powerful symbol of Sai Baba's universal presence and His message of love, compassion, and unity. It reinforces the idea that Sai Baba transcends boundaries and is accessible to all, regardless of caste, creed, or social status. The devotion and togetherness experienced during this ceremony create a sense of community and reinforce the spiritual bond shared among Sai Baba's devotees. Beyond its religious significance, the Pallaki Seva is a vibrant celebration of Sai Baba's teachings and His profound impact on the lives of those who follow His path. It is a beautiful expression of devotion and a reminder that Sai Baba's divine grace is ever-present and accessible to all who seek His blessings.
